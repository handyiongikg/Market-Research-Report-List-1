<p><p>The sanitary membrane filtration market encompasses various membrane types, each with unique properties. Cellulose acetate and cellulose nitrate (collodion) membranes are biocompatible and ideal for low-pressure applications. Polyamide (nylon) membranes offer high chemical resistance, while polycarbonate membranes provide robustness and clarity. Polypropylene membranes are durable and cost-effective, whereas polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) membranes excel in chemical resistance. Ceramic membranes are highly durable and suitable for high-temperature applications, while metal membranes offer strength and stability. Molecular sieve composite membranes enable precise separation, and glass membranes are used for specialized filtration needs.</p></p>
