<p><p>The synthetic rubber-based pressure-sensitive market is primarily categorized into two types: solvent-based and hot-melt types. Solvent-based pressure-sensitive adhesives utilize organic solvents to dissolve rubber polymers, resulting in strong adhesion and flexibility, making them suitable for various applications. In contrast, hot-melt types consist of thermoplastic materials that are melted and applied, offering rapid bonding and environmental benefits due to the absence of solvents. Both types cater to diverse industries, including packaging, automotive, and electronics, each with unique performance characteristics.</p></p>
